Srinivasa Ramanujan

The man who knew infinity

Here's a time line of Srinivasa Ramanujan life:

  • 1887 - Born in Erode, Madras Presidency, British India (present-day Tamil Nadu, India)
  • 1903 - Read Carr's Synopsis and plunges into mathematical exploration
  • 1909 - Married 9-year-old Janaki, begins to seek employment
  • 1913 - Wrote to G.H. Hardy at Cambridge
  • 1914 - Went to England; began to write major papers
  • 1918 - Elected Fellow of the Royal Society and awarded Fellowship of Trinity College, Cambridge
  • 1919 - Returned to India in ill health
  • 1920 - Died in Madras, India of tuberculosis

"In 1918, he was elected as one of the youngest Fellows in the history of Royal Society for his investigation in Elliptic functions and the Theory of Numbers. In the same year, he was the first Indian who was honoured by being elected as a Fellow of Trinity College."
